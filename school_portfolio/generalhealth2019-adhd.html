ADHD

Attention-deficit/hyperactivity disorder (ADHD) is a brain disorder marked by an ongoing pattern of inattention and/or hyperactivity-impulsivity that interferes with functioning or development.

    What are the symptoms of adhd

  • Inattention means a person shifts off task, lacks endurance, has difficulty maintaining attention; also these issues are not caused by defiance or lack of understanding.
  • Hyperactivity means that a person appears to move about always, including in situations in which it is not proper; or extravagantly taps, or talks. In grown-ups, it may be extreme restlessness or wearing others out with regular activity.
  • Impulsivity means an individual makes quick actions that occur at the moment without first thinking about them and that may have a high potential for harm, or a request for instant compensations or inability to delay gratification. An impulsive person may be socially intrusive and extremely interrupt others or make important decisions without considering the long-term consequences.

    Getting Diagnosed

    Doctors check for behavior that's:
  • Not typical for the person’s age. (Most children can behave in those ways at some point or another, though.)
  • Has a negative impact on the person’s ability to function at home, in social environments, or at work.

    • They also have to consistently display at least six of the above symptoms:
  • For at least 6 months
  • And in at least two settings, such as at home and in school

What to do next


Patients that have ADHD can often use medications and do therapy

  • Education for the family
  • Psychological intervention
  • Drug treatment or natural remedies
